Good Story: "Emotion During the Pandemic"

A Short Group Video Exploring Personal Emotions During COVID-19

A reflective storytelling piece built on personal experience, collaborative honesty, and the belief that emotional truth matters more than production polish.

  • Overview
  • Storytelling
  • Production
  • Reflection

Structure

Group Work (3 members)

Role

Team Member

Tool

Smartphone · WeVideo

Timeline

2 Weeks

Responsibility

Content Creation · Video Recording · Editing

Overview

This project is a short group video created as a storytelling exercise focused on personal emotions experienced during the COVID-19 pandemic. The video explores how isolation, uncertainty, and changes in daily life affected us on an emotional level — told through individual perspectives combined into a shared narrative.

The project prioritizes authenticity and personal expression over polished production technique. The constraint of smartphone-only recording was intentional: it kept the focus on what was being said and felt, rather than how it looked. The story carries the video — not the equipment.

Storytelling Approach

The team worked collaboratively to shape a narrative that felt honest without being heavy-handed. Pandemic fatigue was real — the risk of the video feeling like a complaint or a lament was something we actively tried to avoid by focusing on specific, concrete moments rather than broad generalizations about "difficult times."

Each team member contributed personal reflections that were then woven together into a shared arc — moving from the initial disorientation of lockdown, through the small adaptations that became habits, toward a quieter sense of what the experience revealed about what actually matters.

  • Specific moments over general statements

    The most emotionally resonant parts of the video are the small, specific details — a particular routine, a specific day, a feeling with a precise texture. Abstract claims about pandemic hardship distance the viewer; concrete moments invite them in.

  • Multiple voices, one arc

    Three different personal experiences needed to cohere into a single video that felt unified rather than fragmented. The editorial challenge was finding the through-line — the shared emotional progression that made individual stories feel like chapters rather than separate pieces.

Production & Editing

All footage was recorded using smartphones — a constraint that reinforced the documentary feel of the video and matched its emotional register. WeVideo was used for the collaborative edit, allowing all three team members to work on the timeline simultaneously and iterate together on pacing and structure.

  • Constraint as a creative tool

    Limiting production to smartphone footage removed the temptation to hide behind visual quality. It forced every creative decision back to the content itself — what to say, how to frame it, and what to leave out.

  • Pacing for emotional space

    Unlike informational video, emotional storytelling benefits from pauses. The edit was intentionally restrained — letting moments breathe rather than cutting quickly away from them — to give the viewer time to feel rather than just process.

Reflection

This project was a reminder that storytelling skill — knowing what to include, what to cut, and what order creates meaning — is independent of production budget. The tools were minimal. The decisions that mattered were entirely about narrative and emotional truth.

Authenticity is a design choice

Choosing not to over-produce the video was a deliberate decision, not a limitation. The rawness of the footage aligned with the subject matter — and that alignment is part of what makes the video work.

Collaboration requires vulnerability

Sharing personal reflections about difficult emotional experiences with a group required trust. The best content in the video came from the moments the team felt comfortable enough to be genuinely honest.

Editing is where the story becomes real

The footage existed before editing. The story emerged in the edit. Decisions about what order things appeared in, which moments to cut short, and where to let silence sit — those were the decisions that shaped what the video actually said.
