Creating a Short-Form Promotional Video Using AI-Assisted Production

A concise, professionally produced promo built from scratch — from script to final cut — designed to communicate key campus highlights with clarity and warmth for online audiences.

Overview

This project is a short promotional video created for uOttawa Kanata North Campus to support outreach and communication efforts. The video was designed to present key campus information in a clear, engaging, and approachable format suitable for sharing on social media and institutional web platforms.

The full production pipeline — from scripting through final export — was handled independently. AI-based video production software (Renderforest) was used to generate the animated foundation, which was then refined and edited in Final Cut Pro to achieve the pacing and tone appropriate for the campus audience.

Script & Narrative Structure

The script was written entirely from scratch before any production work began. Defining the narrative structure first — what to say, in what order, and at what pace — made every subsequent production decision easier and more intentional.

The core challenge was translating complex institutional information about a campus into content that felt human and accessible in under two minutes. The script was written to be understood on first listen, without relying on viewers reading captions or replaying sections.

  • Clarity over completeness

    A common mistake in institutional video is trying to cover everything. The script was deliberately edited to surface only the three or four facts an interested prospective student most needs — keeping the video tight and the message memorable.

  • Natural pacing

    The script was read aloud multiple times during drafting to test rhythm and natural delivery. Points that required long sentences were restructured into shorter, more punchy statements — improving how the video feels when watched, not just how it reads on paper.

Production & Editing

Renderforest was used to generate the animated template and motion graphics that form the visual backbone of the video. The AI-assisted workflow allowed rapid iteration on visual structure before committing to a final direction. Final Cut Pro was then used for all subsequent editing: timing adjustments, audio integration, pacing refinements, and export optimization.

  • AI-assisted foundation, human-refined finish

    Renderforest accelerated the structural setup — providing motion, transitions, and a consistent visual system without building from scratch. Final Cut Pro handled all the nuance: precise cut points, music sync, and the moments where automated timing needed a human judgment call.

  • Visuals that support, not distract

    Every motion graphic and transition was evaluated against a single question: does this help the viewer absorb what's being said, or does it pull attention away? Animations that competed with the narration were simplified or removed.

Reflection

This project taught me that script quality is the single most important factor in a short-form video — more than animation quality, more than visual polish. A clear, well-paced script makes everything else easier. A weak script can't be saved in post.

Write first, produce second

Starting with a finalized script before touching production tools prevented the most common video production mistake: building visuals that don't quite match the message, then trying to retrofit the script to fit what was already built.

AI tools expand what's achievable solo

Using Renderforest for the visual foundation made it possible to produce a professionally structured video independently — without a motion design team. Understanding how to direct AI tools effectively is now part of the production skill set.

Institutional content can feel human

The challenge with organizational promotional video is avoiding the corporate tone that makes viewers tune out. Writing for spoken delivery rather than formal prose made the video feel approachable without sacrificing credibility.
